Semester Project Summary

I am slightly changing the content for my summester project. My original plan was to create a website that allows both beginner and experienced artists to share or learn other methods of creating art. It would be a community-based platform where people could upload their artistic process and share it with others, or someone could be looking to learn a new medium or artstyle so they would search for a tutorial to follow on my website. However, I realized that making an upload feature might be too difficult for me at this point in my knowledge of coding, so I have decided to condense my idea into a simpler framework. My new plan is for the website to have pre-loaded tutorials on the site that are step-by-step with images followed by a transcript. The user will still be able to search through a list of art mediums like painting, drawing, or sculpture. From there, they can filter what specific style they want to learn, and then there would be 3 different "levels" for each style: Beginner, Intermediate, and Experienced. This is sort of a "test your skill" type of system, where beginners can start from the basics and work their way up, while the pros can choose more advanced tutorials. I plan to give each medium 3 or 4 different styles to give the sense that there is variety. I will also do research on what specific styles are most commonly practiced/sought after by artist so that I can put them in my website.

Six Thinking Hats:

White Hat

The information that I have for my project is the idea: the problem, the solutions, the website layout, and the website content. The information that I need is what forms of art are likely to be studied, and what art styles are popular amongst learners. I can get this information from research and polls from fellow artists, beginners, and friends.

Red Hat

I want to slightly alter my idea for this project. Instead of making a community-based platform where anyone can upload their personal art techniques and routines, I will put certain art tutorials based on what is most searched or most popular with the general art community. There will be a smaller variety of styles and media than I initially planned. I am scared that my original idea is too complex and time-consuming, so I am slightly altering the idea. I am excited to execute my idea, but I just worry that the result will not look like what I had hoped for while in the planning stages.

Black Hat

What doesn’t fit in with my project is the scale of content I had planned to implement on the website. The problem is that I had too large an idea, so I need to condense it. Potential problems I could run into are not finding accurate data for my website. A possible downside of my project is the content, but hopefully it works out.

Yellow Hat

This new idea might work because it still achieves the goal of providing a space for artists to learn AND improve their skills. To make this work, I must appeal to artists of every skill level, while still having a variety of tutorials for everyone to explore. I can make this more attractive to users by using content that is highly sought out by artists.

Green Hat

Other alternatives for this project could include video tutorials or written instructions along with photos of each tutorial. I am still doing research for the project, but I will add to this list once I discover other alternate solutions.

Blue Hat

The subject is art, specifically art tutorials and improving artistic skills. The goal is to create a platform for artists of all skill ranges to learn other forms and styles of art at any skill level. To keep my project on track, I will keep the project goals in mind and continue to ask myself, “Does this align with the project?” I will also take notes on my research, test the alternatives, and rework my solutions when problems occur.
